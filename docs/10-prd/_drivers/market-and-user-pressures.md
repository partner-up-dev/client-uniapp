# Market and user pressures

## Find partners with low friction

Users need a fast path to discover relevant partner requests and decide whether to join.

Tradeoff:

- The browse flow must stay lightweight, but enough structure is needed for users to understand roles, routes, timing, and participation expectations.

## Reduce ambiguity in joining

Partnering fails when users do not know what role they are applying for, why they are eligible, or what approval means.

Tradeoff:

- The product needs role-specific application structure without making the join flow feel heavyweight.

## Keep active partners coordinated

After interest or participation starts, users need direct communication and visible activity updates.

Tradeoff:

- Messaging and notification surfaces should support coordination without becoming a separate social product.

## Fit mini-program usage

The client primarily targets Weixin and Alipay mini-program contexts.

Tradeoff:

- Product behavior should assume platform constraints, compact flows, and mobile-first sessions.

## Support multi-locale copy

User-facing copy needs locale support so product surfaces can be operated consistently across supported languages.

Tradeoff:

- Locale structure should reduce copy drift without blocking product iteration.
